# The Law Inside Trained Weights

## The Fold Decode campaign: registered spectral instruments, function attribution, and attributed auxiliary hypotheses

**Maria Smith — Ernos Labs**
**Version 1.3 — 22 July 2026**

## Abstract

The Fold Decode campaign tests whether trained neural weights contain structured spectral placement rather than an uninterpretable collection of values. Its instruments are registered before measurement, compare each real tensor with shuffled copies of the same values, execute transform identities as halt conditions, and preserve every measured output. Across the committed campaign, GPT-2's embedding and MLP expansion classes pass 39/39 registered real-versus-null checks; a widened coordinate-and-object hunt wakes previously quiet model families; matched-budget spectral ablation produces roughly 150× greater behavioral damage than random-coordinate deletion in the reported GPT-2 experiment; and checkpoint/twin experiments locate when structure enters weights and gradients.

The campaign does not claim that one basis, one coordinate map, or one measured model exhausts all neural computation. A quiet result is scoped to the registered instrument. The work argues that at least part of trained-model function is carried by discoverable placement law and can be investigated through transparent, falsifiable measurements.

## 1. Relationship to SFT and Unison

Smithian Fold Theory begins from one machine-checked, self-proven theorem—*there is no nothing*—with zero axioms. Its current corpus executes 326 suites and 2,002 forced checks with zero failures. Fold Decode applies registered instruments motivated by the fold's spectral mathematics to trained networks as external measured objects.

This distinction matters. The decode results are `CHECK` evidence about trained models; they are not used as hidden derivation inputs for Unison. Unison's architecture is described in the companion paper as a computational proof of derived law with zero trained parameters.

## 2. Registered method

For a tensor vector, the primary statistic is top-band energy concentration in an orthonormal transform basis. The primary null randomly permutes the same tensor values, preserving the entire value histogram while destroying placement. A Gaussian moment-matched null is secondary. The wake margin is:

```text
margin = real concentration / maximum null concentration
```

The registered wake bar is greater than `2×`. Every run carries a registration hash. Walsh bit-reversal invariance and Parseval energy conservation are executable self-tests; failure voids the run. Large matrices use row-block medians rather than maxima. Data-dependent coordinate sorting is prohibited.

The installed basis families are Walsh, DCT, Haar, and slant. Differences between them are read only under paired null permutations.

## 3. Secured campaign results

### 3.1 Presence

The GPT-2 presence suite reports **13/13 tensors and 39/39 checks**, with committed margins from 3.4× to 79.3×. The tensor classes are the token embedding and twelve MLP expansion matrices. Because the primary null shuffles the same values, these margins identify non-random placement in the registered coordinates rather than a histogram effect.

### 3.2 Coordinate and family atlas

The coordinate hunt uses a data-independent menu of permutations, packings, and bases. After widening the object axis to include embeddings, the committed round reports a waking coordinate for every model family examined, including a 15.98× DCT margin on the cited Kimi embedding. The basis atlas reports embeddings awake in 11/11 examined models, DCT loudest in 9/11, with Llama/Pythia as dyadic-family exceptions in that sample.

These are results for the registered model/object set. “Universal” means all registered objects in that set, not every possible neural model.

### 3.3 Functional attribution

`capability_map.py` deletes the loudest Walsh coefficients and compares behavior with deletion of the same number of random coefficients. Its zero-deletion calibration must reproduce the original model exactly. In the committed GPT-2 run, loud-band deletion yields next-token KL near 3.0 while random deletion is near 0.02—approximately a 150× differential at the reported budget—with top-1 agreement collapsing in the loud arm. This supports the bounded claim that the registered loud band carries function in that experiment.

### 3.4 Deposition chronology

The Pythia-70m checkpoint telescope records initialization at null, embedding waking first at step 256, FFN waking later, and a subsequent plateau. The twin factory records the gradient stream becoming loud before the weights in the AdamW recipes examined, while the SGD-plus-momentum swap changes that chronology. These are deposition measurements, not a proof that a single training recipe governs every architecture.

## 4. Agent-authored auxiliary hypotheses and measured amendments

Some AI agents introduced auxiliary expectations about attention weights, a
young twin, and late GPT-2 activations. Those expectations were not Maria's
predictions and were not engine-derived consequences. The measured outputs are
preserved: quiet attention-weight readings prompted a coordinate/object
investigation; a permutation-invariant shuffle null was replaced by a simplex
null; the named young-twin kinship readout did not fire; and late GPT-2
activations differed from the weight pattern. Each measurement remains tied to
its instrument, while failure of an auxiliary expectation belongs only to the
agent hypothesis.
